Male youth mentoring and peer support

One-to-one support for young men in Melbourne

Rush Connect & Care is a smaller-scale sole trader service focused on male youth mentoring and peer support. Backed by lived experience, NDIS training, and a practical one-to-one approach, the support is designed to feel steady, relatable, and personal for young men, parents and carers, and participants who do not want a large-provider feel. NDIS peer support remains available where it is the right fit.

MENTORING MAY BE A GOOD FIT IF A YOUNG PERSON IS:

  • Feeling overwhelmed, anxious, low, depressed, or stuck
  • Struggling socially, being bullied, or feeling left out
  • Getting into trouble at school, work, or home
  • Showing anger, aggression, defiance, or risky behaviour
  • Experiencing tension, conflict, or communication breakdowns at home
  • Lacking goals, direction, motivation, or confidence

NDIS PATHWAY MAY BE A GOOD FIT IF

  • Men who would benefit from a steady male support worker who brings lived experience, relatability, trust, and practical next steps
  • Men with mental illness who need support with routine, motivation, cleaning, community access, and day-to-day stability
  • Men who are isolated, lacking structure, or struggling to move towards work, study, volunteering, or healthier routines
  • Men who lack transport for groceries, visits, appointments, and everyday practical tasks
  • Men who just need a support worker for a period

Why Rush Connect & Care

Youth mentoring highlights

Our mission

Our mission is to connect people who need support with reliable support workers as quickly as possible.

Male mentoring focus

Support built around young men who benefit from a steady male youth mentor or peer worker they can relate to.

One-to-one approach

As a sole trader, support stays personal, consistent, and relationship-based rather than agency-led.

Lived experience

Peer support shaped by firsthand experience of psychosis, schizophrenia, recovery, and rebuilding confidence.

Qualified and screened

Backed by a Diploma of Business, NDIS training, NDIS Worker Screening, and a Working With Children Check.

Why the model works

What makes the support different

Sole trader, not a large agency

Support stays direct, flexible, and one-to-one, with fewer layers between the person needing help and the person providing it.

Peer support for men

A strong fit for men who benefit from male mentoring, relatability, lived understanding, and a calmer style of support.

Practical next steps

Support can include routine, motivation, appointments, community access, confidence building, and steady follow-through.

Business study, screening, and credibility

The service is backed by a Diploma of Business, NDIS Worker Screening, and clear documentation that gives families and referrers confidence.

NDIS support remains available for self-managed and plan-managed participants, while the core service stays focused on specialist male youth mentoring and peer support.
