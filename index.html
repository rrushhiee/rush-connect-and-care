Online support for young people

1:1 Online support helping overwhelmed youth feel better.

Having experienced my own challenges as a troubled young person, I bring lived understanding alongside a steady, non-judgmental presence. I aim to be someone young people can trust: consistent, grounded, and genuinely in their corner.

Whether it is a young person feeling lost or overwhelmed, or a parent in distress trying to support their child, it can make a real difference having someone who understands, can empathise, and is consistently in your corner to help make things a little less tough. Sessions focus on building confidence, emotional support, structure and routine, motivation, and practical next steps toward a more stable and positive direction.

NDIS support for men remains available as a smaller part of the service.

THIS MAY BE A GOOD FIT IF

  • A young person would benefit from steady support without a clinical feel
  • A parent or carer is in crisis with their adolescent
  • A parent is worried their child feels lost, is acting out, or is struggling with growing up
  • Online sessions would make it easier to show up consistently

Private online mentoring is the main focus. NDIS can still be discussed where it is a strong fit.

Mentoring focus

What online youth mentoring can support

Emotional support

Regular check-ins for young people navigating stress, anxiety, low mood, or feeling overwhelmed.

Routine and motivation

Support with structure, follow-through, and building habits that make daily life feel more manageable.

Confidence and connection

Encouragement around self-belief, friendships and parental-checkins to ensure progress is made.

Growing into adulthood

Practical support for young people figuring out direction, responsibility, and the next steps into adult life.

Parents, carers, and referrers are welcome to make the first contact when a young person is not ready to reach out themselves. Parent and carer check-ins are available at no extra cost.
