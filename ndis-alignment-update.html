Update

How Rush Connect and Care stays aligned with NDIS updates

Published 25 April 2026 · Updated 29 April 2026

At Rush Connect and Care (RCC), quality and compliance are not one-time tasks. They are part of how we deliver safe, respectful, and person-centred support every day.

This update helps NDIS participants, families, plan managers, and support coordinators understand how a small Melbourne peer support service keeps quality, boundaries, and safe support practice visible.

Back to updates View resources

The NDIS sector continues to evolve, with updates to quality expectations, safeguarding requirements, provider obligations, and best-practice guidance. RCC stays aligned by actively monitoring updates from the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission and the NDIA, and by regularly reviewing our internal policies, procedures, and staff practices.

This matters because NDIS providers and workers are expected to deliver supports in line with the NDIS Code of Conduct, which promotes safe, ethical, and respectful services for people with disability. The NDIS Practice Standards also outline the quality standards expected of registered providers delivering supports and services to NDIS participants.

Continuous improvement

How RCC supports quality and compliance in practice

Regular policy and procedure reviews

We update our internal systems to reflect changes in NDIS requirements, practice expectations, and safeguarding guidance.

Ongoing staff training and supervision

Our team is supported to understand their responsibilities, including safe service delivery, participant rights, dignity, choice, and control.

Participant-focused quality checks

We use feedback, complaints, incident reviews, and service monitoring to identify what is working well and where we can improve.

Commitment to safe and ethical supports

We align our work with the principles of the NDIS Code of Conduct, including respect, integrity, prevention of harm, and responsive support.

Monitoring sector updates and best practice

The NDIS Commission publishes guidance, including practice alerts that help providers understand safe and best-practice service delivery. RCC uses this type of information to keep improving how we support participants.

For participants, families, and support coordinators, this means you can feel confident that RCC is committed to keeping pace with NDIS changes, strengthening our services, and maintaining high standards of care.

Our goal is simple: to provide supports that are safe, reliable, respectful, and aligned with current NDIS quality and safeguarding expectations.
