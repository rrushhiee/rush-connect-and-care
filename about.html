About

Lived Experience Support that Understands

Rush Connect & Care is built around lived understanding, practical support, and a calm approach that feels real and relatable for young people, families and men suffering from mental challenges.

Rupert's mentoring and support background

Having gone through my own difficult years as a young person, I bring more than just professional interest to this work. I understand how much it can matter to have someone steady, non-judgmental, and genuinely in your corner—especially when a young person feels lost, overwhelmed, disconnected, or unsure of where they are heading.

Rush Connect & Care is mainly focused on youth mentoring. I support young people by building trust first, then working alongside them with confidence, emotional support, routine, motivation, and practical next steps. The aim is not to lecture or “fix” someone, but to help them feel less alone, more understood, and more able to move toward a stable and positive direction. I also understand how distressing it can be for parents and carers who are trying their best to support a young person and do not always know what else to do.

Alongside youth mentoring, I also provide NDIS support for men as a smaller but important part of the service. This support is practical, calm, and goal-focused, helping with things like routine, community access, social connection, appointments, daily structure, and building confidence over time. My approach is grounded in respect, consistency, and meeting people where they are.

I hold a Working With Children Check, First Aid Certificate, and NDIS Worker Screening. I also hold a Diploma of Business and have completed studies in Mental Health and Community Services, which supports a practical and well-rounded approach to mentoring and support work.

Support style

Support is designed to feel relatable and practical rather than clinical or disconnected.
