About

Lived Experience Youth Support that Understands

Rush Connect & Care is built around lived understanding, practical support, and a calm approach that feels real and relatable for young people and families.

Having experienced my own challenges as a troubled young person, I bring lived understanding alongside a steady, non-judgmental presence. I aim to be someone young people can trust—consistent, grounded, and genuinely in their corner. I hold a Working With Children Check (WWCC) and NDIS Worker Screening.

Whether it’s a young person feeling lost or overwhelmed, or a parent in distress trying to support their child, it can make a real difference having someone who understands, can empathise, and is consistently in your corner to help make things a little less tough. Sessions focus on building confidence, emotional support, structure and routine, motivation, and practical next steps toward a more stable and positive direction.

Alongside lived experience, I hold a Diploma of Business and have completed studies in Mental Health and Community Services, supporting a practical and well-rounded approach to mentoring.

NDIS support for men is also available as a smaller part of the service.
