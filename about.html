Get to know me

About Rupert and the story behind Rush Connect & Care

I am Rupert, the person behind Rush Connect & Care. As I tell you this, the service is based on lived experience. Going through a psychosis at 17, I had a lot of trouble with the thoughts I was having. Fast-forward 6 years and I am diagnosed with schizophrenia. As I get older, it becomes clearer that these mental illness diagnoses have pushed me towards my potential and figuring myself out, and how I operate. It is entering a state of change.

A big part of what I bring is understanding that people do not always need someone to come in with all the answers straight away. Sometimes they need someone who can sit with where they are at, be steady, and help make things feel more manageable. I think trust, patience, and consistency matter a lot, and I try to bring that into the way I support people. I know life can get messy, and I know how much it can help when someone is genuinely in your corner.

The things I like doing consist of entrepreneurship, owning a successful business, building a broad network, and constantly educating myself on new tech or the latest news. I play Aussie Rules football for a club in Alphington and basketball at Richmond’s YMCA just behind Citizen Park. I have found the concept of fitness, a simple term, so essential to my health, although I am not always on a big run or at the gym. I like to read and have read various books, but my favourite was Greenlights by Matthew McConaughey. He covers different life events and paints the picture of waiting for green lights as a representation of knowing the right time to go for it at certain times in your life. My favourite movie was Star Wars, the first M-rated movie I saw, shown to me by a family friend. I enjoy practising mindfulness and time for silence. In my mindfulness, I meditate mostly, which flows into my everyday life.

I also value community, growth, and being around people who are trying to better themselves in one way or another. Playing sport, staying active, learning new things, and building something meaningful through Rush Connect & Care all give me a sense of direction. I think those things help keep me grounded. They also remind me that progress does not have to be perfect to still be real.

I think you can expect from me a genuine, driven person who wants to get the best outcome possible for the client: calm, supportive, available, able to build a healthy relationship, and practical in navigating everyday life.

To me, my work matters because I can work on something I am passionate about, and I am grateful to be able to do it.
