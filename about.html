About

Lived experience support that understands

Rush Connect & Care is a sole trader service built around lived experience, specialist male youth mentoring, and one-to-one peer support for young men, families, and men navigating mental health challenges.

Get to know me

Get to know Rupert

I am Rupert, the person behind Rush Connect & Care. The service is built on lived experience. When I went through psychosis at 17, I had a lot of trouble with the thoughts I was having. Fast-forward six years, and I was diagnosed with schizophrenia. As I get older, it becomes clearer that these diagnoses have pushed me towards my potential and helped me figure out who I am and how I operate. It has all been part of an ongoing process of change.

A big part of what I bring is understanding that people do not always need someone to come in with all the answers straight away. Sometimes they just need someone who gets it, without judgement or acting like an authority over them. I think trust, patience, and consistency matter a lot, and I try to bring that into the way I support people. I know life can get messy, and I know how much it can help when someone is genuinely in your corner.

Alongside lived experience, I bring formal study through a Diploma of Business and completed training in NDIS both at work and through completion of training modules. I also hold a Working With Children Check, First Aid Certificate, and NDIS Worker Screening, which helps keep the work grounded, credible, and safe.

The things I enjoy include entrepreneurship, running a successful business, building a broad network, and constantly educating myself on new tech and the latest news. I play Aussie Rules football for a club in Alphington and basketball at Richmond’s YMCA, just behind Citizen Park. I have found fitness, simple as it sounds, to be essential to my health, even though I am not always on a big run or at the gym. I like to read and have read various books, but my favourite is Greenlights by Matthew McConaughey. He covers different life events and uses green lights as a way of representing knowing the right time to go for it at different moments in life. My favourite movie is Star Wars, the first M-rated movie I saw, shown to me by a family friend. I enjoy practising mindfulness and making time for silence. I meditate mostly, which is another essential practice for everyday life and mental health.

I also value community, growth, and being around people who are trying to better themselves in one way or another. Playing sport, staying active, learning new things, and building something meaningful through Rush Connect & Care all give me a sense of direction. I also volunteer at various community health centres because it is something I find rewarding. I think those things help keep me grounded. They also remind me that progress does not have to be perfect and that, with time, necessary changes happen or we learn to adapt.

I think you can expect me to be genuine and driven, while also being calm, supportive, available, able to build a healthy relationship, and practical in navigating everyday life.

To me, my work matters because I can work on something I am passionate about, and I am grateful to be able to do it.
