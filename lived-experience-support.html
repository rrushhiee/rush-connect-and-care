Why Lived Experience Support Can Make a Real Difference for Clients

Published 28 April 2026

When someone is looking for support, they are not just looking for a worker who can complete tasks. They are often looking for someone who can listen, understand, encourage, and walk beside them through real-life challenges.

Back to updates Start an enquiry

This is where lived experience support can be incredibly powerful. Lived experience means a support worker has personally faced challenges such as mental health struggles, recovery, isolation, life transitions, or personal hardship.

While every person's journey is different, lived experience can help create a deeper level of understanding, trust, and connection between the support worker and the client.

At Rush Connect and Care, we believe support should feel genuine, respectful, and human.

What it means

What is lived experience support?

Lived experience support is when a worker uses their own personal insight, growth, and recovery journey in a professional and respectful way to support others.

It does not mean making the support session about the worker. Instead, it means the worker can draw on real understanding to better relate to the client's emotions, challenges, and goals.

For many clients, especially men who may find it difficult to open up, this can make support feel safer, more relatable, and less clinical.

Benefits

How lived experience can help

Clients feel truly understood

Clients can feel less judged when support comes from someone who understands that progress is not always simple or linear.

It builds trust faster

When a client knows their worker has faced challenges too, the relationship can feel more equal, honest, and human.

It brings hope

A support worker with lived experience can show that difficult times do not have to be the end of the story.

Support becomes practical

Lived experience recognises that getting out, attending appointments, rebuilding routine, and asking for help can be major steps.

It reduces stigma

Support can send the message that struggling does not make someone weak, broken, or less valuable.

The person comes first

The client is treated as a whole person with strengths, goals, interests, challenges, and potential.

It can be especially helpful for men

Many men find it difficult to talk about mental health, emotions, or personal challenges. Some may feel pressure to be strong, independent, or silent.

Lived experience support can help break down those barriers. When support feels genuine and relatable, men may be more willing to engage, have honest conversations, build routines, reconnect with community, and work toward personal goals.

This is why Rush Connect and Care focuses on providing genuine 1:1 support for men in Melbourne.

Lived experience still needs professional boundaries

Lived experience is powerful, but it works best when combined with professionalism, respect, confidentiality, and clear boundaries.

A good lived experience support worker does not overshare or take over the client's journey. They use their experience carefully to support the client's needs. The focus always remains on the client, their goals, their choices, and their wellbeing.

Final thoughts

Genuine understanding can change the support relationship

Lived experience support can make support feel more real, more comfortable, and more meaningful. It can help build trust, reduce shame, encourage hope, and create practical steps toward independence and confidence.

If you or someone you support is looking for genuine 1:1 NDIS support for men in Melbourne, Rush Connect and Care is here to help.
