NDIS funding guide

Using your NDIS plan with Rush Connect & Care

If you are self-managed or plan-managed, getting started is simple. NDIS support for men remains available as a smaller part of the service, while online youth mentoring continues as the main focus.

Plan types

How funding can work

Self-managed participants

  • You choose the provider you want to work with
  • Invoices are paid directly and claimed through your plan
  • Support can stay flexible around your goals

Plan-managed participants

  • Invoices can be sent to your plan manager
  • The admin stays clearer and more straightforward
  • Support can stay aligned with your goals and plan

Simple process

What getting started looks like

1

Reach out

Send a short enquiry about the participant, goals, and the kind of support you are looking for.

2

Confirm the fit

We can discuss whether the support needed is a good match and how funding would work.

3

Begin support

Once the fit is clear, support can begin in a calm and practical way that suits the participant.

Helpful details

What to include in your enquiry

  • Whether the plan is self-managed or plan-managed
  • The participant's suburb or general area
  • A short outline of goals or the support needed
  • Any preferred days, times, or contact method
  • Anything important about the participant's routine or current challenges

Need a starting point?

Send a simple enquiry

A short message is enough to begin. If you are actually looking for private online youth mentoring instead, you can use the same enquiry form.
